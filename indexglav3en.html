World Olympic Torch Relay



This is a tradition held before every Olympic Games. This tradition first appeared in Berlin in 1936.
The Olympic flame, lit with a parabolic concave mirror from the sun's rays in Greece, was brought to Berlin with the help of three thousand three hundred and thirty-one runner-athletes. Thus, having covered a distance of more than three thousand kilometers.



Unusual transportation methods
Runners usually take part in the Olympic Torch Relay, but other means of transportation are also used. For example: in 1952, the delivery of fire by plane and cross-country skiing were included in the relay; in 1988 - delivery by snowmobile; in 1992 - delivery by supersonic aircraft; in 2002 - delivery by dog sleds, sleighs and snowmobiles, in 2006 - delivery in a Ferrari team car and a Venetian gondola.
